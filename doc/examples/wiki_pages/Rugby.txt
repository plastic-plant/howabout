'''Zachary''' "'''Zack'''" '''Test''' (born October 13, 1989) is a retired American [[rugby union]] player who played for the [[United States national rugby sevens team]]. With 143 career tries in the [[World Rugby Sevens Series]], Test led all U.S. players in tries scored until [[Perry Baker]] broke his record in early 2018. He is the current head coach of the [[San Diego Legion]] in [[Major League Rugby]] (MLR).

==Early life and youth rugby==
Test was born in [[Woodside, California]], and lived in [[Redwood City]]. He is Jewish and went to Ronald C. Wornick Jewish Day School in [[Foster City]].<ref name="jweekly.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.jweekly.com/2016/07/29/local-jewish-day-school-grad-on-u-s-olympic-rugby-team/|title = Local Jewish day school alum on U.S. Olympic rugby team|date = 29 July 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.maccabiusa.com/news/press-releases/586-maccabi-usa-fields-an-elite-level-open-rugby-team-for-the-maccabiah-games.html |title=Maccabi Usa Fields An Elite Level Open Rugby Team For The Maccabiah Games |publisher=Maccabiusa.com |access-date=August 20, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.erugbynews.com/article.php?sec=116&a=5002 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090918124458/http://www.erugbynews.com/article.php?sec=116&a=5002 |url-status=usurped |archive-date=September 18, 2009 |title=eRugbyNews.com |publisher=eRugbyNews.com |date=June 19, 2009 |access-date=August 20, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thejc.com/sport/sport-news/team-gb-rugby-through-gold-medal-match |title=Team GB rugby through to gold medal match |publisher=The Jewish Chronicle |date=July 20, 2009 |access-date=August 20, 2013}}</ref><ref name="go">{{cite web|url=http://espn.go.com/college-football/player/stats/_/id/238471/zach-test |title=Zach Test Stats |publisher=ESPN |date=October 13, 1989 |access-date=August 20, 2013}}</ref>    
He attended [[Woodside High School (Woodside, California)|Woodside High School]], graduating in 2007.<ref name="go"/><ref>{{cite news| url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=OKTB&p_theme=oktb&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=119C97F0B9B3AEC8&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM | work=Oakland Tribune, The | title=Woodside High School 2007 Graduates | date=June 14, 2007}}</ref> Test began playing rugby in his freshman year of high school.

Test began his collegiate career playing American football in 2007 at the [[University of Oregon]] as a wide receiver under [[Mike Bellotti]], where he was a preferred walk-on but did not see playing time and red-shirted. Test also played rugby for the [[University of Oregon]] helping them qualify for the regional playoffs.
After his freshman year, Test transferred to [[Loughborough University]].

Test also represented his country with the national under-20 rugby union team.

==U.S. national rugby sevens team==
Test made his sevens senior debut during the [[2008–09 IRB Sevens World Series]] and represented the United States at the [[2009 Hong Kong Sevens|Hong Kong]], [[2009 Adelaide Sevens|Adelaide]] and [[2009 London Sevens|London]] Sevens that year. Test quickly became a regular for the U.S. team, playing for the US in every tournament in the [[IRB Sevens World Series]] during the 2009–10 season.<ref name="universalsports1">Universal Sports, Zack Test bio, http://www.universalsports.com/rugby/athletes/athlete=267557504/bio/index.html</ref>
Test also played for the U.S. in every tournament in the [[IRB Sevens World Series]] during the 2010–11 season. T.<ref name="universalsports1"/> Test led the U.S. team during the [[2010-11 IRB Sevens World Series]] with 24 tries and 120 points, scoring 6 tries at the [[2011 Hong Kong Sevens]]. Test also played for the US at the [[United States at the 2011 Pan American Games|2011 Pan American Games]], helping the U.S. team to win the bronze medal.<ref>[[United States at the 2011 Pan American Games#Rugby sevens]]</ref> Test's performance throughout 2011 for the US national team earned him the Rugby Mag 2011 Men's 7s Player of the Year award.<ref>Rugby Mag, 2011 RUGBYMag Awards!, Jan. 1, 2012, http://rugbymag.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=3027:2011-rugbymag-awards&catid=122:rugbymag-reader-news&Itemid=347</ref>

Test quickly emerged as one of the best players on the U.S. team. During the [[2011-12 IRB Sevens World Series]], Test broke [[Jovesa Naivalu]]'s U.S. national sevens career record of 47 tries.<ref>{{cite web|title=A Victory! USA Takes Care of Scotland|url=http://www.rugbymag.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=3902:a-victory-usa-takes-care-of-scotland&catid=45:usa-sevens-men&Itemid=202}}</ref>  Test was also one of the best on the IRB circuit in catching kicks from restarts, using his 6'3" height, his jumping ability and his excellent hands to haul in the high ball. Test had a reputation as one of the best players in the game at catching restarts.<ref>Rugby Magazine Year in Review 2011, https://rugbymag.com/rugbymagazine-dig/Rugby%20Magazine%20Year%20In%20Review%202011/files/assets/seo/page14.html</ref> [[Paul Treu]], head coach of the [[South Africa national rugby sevens team|South Africa rugby team]], has stated that when it comes to catching kicks, Test is "one of the best players in the business."<ref>Sevens: Uncovered – The Aerial Battle, http://www.irbsevens.com/video/index.html?videoid=2061082</ref> Test finished the 2011–12 World Series leading the United States with 21 tries.<ref>{{cite web|title=Statistics|url=http://www.irbsevens.com/statistics/triesdetails.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101121011532/http://www.irbsevens.com/statistics/triesdetails.html|url-status=usurped|archive-date=November 21, 2010}}</ref>

==USA Eagles==
Test debuted for the Eagles 15's side in the [[2015 World Rugby Pacific Nations Cup|2015 Pacific Nations Cup]] against Samoa.<ref>{{cite web|title=United States of America (3) 16 – 21 (21) Samoa (FT)|url=http://en.espn.co.uk/scrum/rugby/match/257955.html|website=espnscrum|access-date=July 19, 2015}}</ref> Test picked up a head injury at the [[2017 Americas Rugby Championship]], requiring him to leave the match and obtain medical treatment. After sitting out of rugby for several months without playing, Test announced his retirement from the game in early 2018.

==Other international==
Test, who is Jewish, played for the United States Maccabiah rugby union team at the [[2009 Maccabi Games]],<ref>{{cite news |title=USA Maccabiah Team Ready For Jewish Olympics |url=http://www.erugbynews.com/article.php?sec=116&a=5002 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090918124458/http://www.erugbynews.com/article.php?sec=116&a=5002 |url-status=usurped |archive-date=September 18, 2009 |publisher=eRugbyNews.com |date=June 19, 2009 |access-date=August 11, 2009 }}</ref> and was instrumental in helping the squad win the bronze medal beating the [[Israel national rugby union team]] at [[Herzliya Municipal Stadium]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Maccabiah USA Team Wins Bronze |url=http://www.erugbynews.com/article.php?sec=116&a=5167 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110412080614/http://erugbynews.com/article.php?sec=116&a=5167 |url-status=usurped |archive-date=April 12, 2011 |publisher=eRugbyNews.com |date=July 22, 2009 |access-date=August 11, 2009 }}</ref>
